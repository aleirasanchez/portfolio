Wind Turbine Blade Design

As part of our final project for the Fluids and Heat Transfer Lab, our team was tasked with designing a blade for a wind turbine. We created a 6-inch blade based on the NACA 4412 airfoil, optimizing chord length and pitch angle to maximize power output. The design process involved calculating the Reynolds number, maximizing the CL/CD ratio, and performing stress and torque analyses to ensure structural integrity. We then conducted experimental testing across multiple wind speeds and RPMs to determine the bladeâ€™s maximum power output and corresponding operating conditions.

  • Ensuring structural integrity of the blade under varying wind speeds while optimizing for maximum power output.

Skills Developed
