Water Pump Project

This was a collaborative project for Cornell's Mechanical Synthesis course. The objective of was to design a water pump capable of lifting water at least 1.5 m above the drive shaft at a rate of 1 L/min. Our team researched four types of pumps (piston, gear, centrifugal, and peristaltic) analyzing functional decompositions, morphological charts, parts, calculations, and potential design sketches. Considering customer requirements, budget, and simplicity, we selected a lift piston pump for its efficiency, consistent flow rate, and straightforward design and manufacturing.

Challenges

  • Ensuring precise tolerances between the piston head and chamber while machining, particularly during slot milling where stability issues led to slight scoring and slanted cuts.
  • Translating the rotational motion of the motor into smooth linear motion of the piston while maintaining reliable suction and consistent water flow.

Skills Developed

  • Applied mechanical design principles, including understanding the mode of action of a piston pump and translating rotational motion into linear motion via a crank mechanism.
  • Gained hands-on experience with machining and fabrication, addressing tolerance challenges, and problem-solving during manufacturing to ensure pump functionality.
