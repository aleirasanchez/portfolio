Actuated Butterfly Tiles for Adaptive Thermal Regulation

As part of my Master of Engineering project at Cornell University, I designed and fabricated soft, butterfly-shaped silicone tiles actuated using shape memory alloys (SMAs) for adaptive thermal regulation. The butterfly geometry enables large surface-area changes with minimal edge motion, making it well-suited for airflow and heat control. The project evolved from an initial rigid mechanical linkage to direct SMA actuation after testing revealed poor compatibility between rigid components and flexible substrates. Using EcoFlex silicone and Nitinol SMA springs, I fabricated and tested multiple prototypes to evaluate electrical, thermal, and mechanical performance. While fully reversible motion was not achieved, the work identified key constraints and design trade-offs for integrating SMAs into soft, adaptive systems.

Challenges

  • Integrating actuation mechanisms with highly compliant materials without introducing stress concentrations or excessive stiffness
  • Managing SMA thermal behavior, including overheating, heat loss to silicone, and permanent loss of trained shape
  • Achieving reliable and reversible motion in a system with minimal passive restoring forces

Skills Developed

  • Soft robotics and smart materials integration using shape memory alloys
  • Iterative mechanical design informed by experimental failure analysis
  • Silicone molding, CAD design, and prototype fabrication
  • Electrical characterization and actuation testing using bench power supplies and multimeters
